**Last Updated:** January 14, 2026

**QuickFix Games** ("the App") is developed and owned by **Gal Itach** ("we", "us", or "our"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your information is collected, used, and disclosed when you use our mobile application.

By downloading and using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

## 1. Information We Collect

### A. Data Collected Automatically (Third Parties)
We do not operate a proprietary server or backend database. However, the App utilizes third-party services that may collect information used to identify you or your device to provide functionality (such as advertisements and leaderboards).

The third-party service providers used by the App include:

*   **Google AdMob:** Used to display advertisements. Google may use your device's Advertising ID (IDFA) to show personalized ads based on your interests.
*   **Apple Game Center:** Used for leaderboards and achievements. Apple collects data regarding your game activity.
*   **Apple StoreKit:** Used for In-App Purchases.

### B. Data Stored Locally
The App stores game progress (scores, Bit-Bot level, unlocked items, currency) locally on your device using Apple's `UserDefaults` and `AppStorage`. This data is not transmitted to us and remains on your device unless you choose to back it up via iCloud.

## 2. Device Permissions

To function correctly, QuickFix Games requires access to specific device features:

*   **Motion & Fitness (Accelerometer/Gyroscope):** The App uses motion sensors for gameplay mechanics (e.g., "Tilt" and "Shake" games). This motion data is processed in real-time on your device and is **never** recorded, stored, or transmitted to any server.
*   **App Tracking Transparency (ATT):** On iOS 14.5+, we request permission to track your activity across other companies' apps and websites. This is strictly for delivering relevant advertisements via Google AdMob. You may decline this permission and still play the game.
